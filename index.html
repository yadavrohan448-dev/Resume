Working Papers and Conference Presentations

Working Papers

1. Targeted Electricity Subsidies and Rural Development: Evidence from India’s RGGVY Program
This paper studies whether targeted electricity subsidies can improve rural welfare outcomes in India. It examines the Rajiv Gandhi Grameen Vidyutikaran Yojana (RGGVY), which provided free electricity connections to Below Poverty Line (BPL) households. Using India Human Development Survey panel data for 2004–05 and 2011–12 and district-level variation in RGGVY rollout, the paper applies a difference-in-differences design based on years of exposure to the programme. The findings suggest that RGGVY increased electricity connections and ownership of basic electric appliances among BPL households, with gains in income, consumption expenditure, non-agricultural income, non-farm work, and basic educational outcomes.

2. Rural Electrification and the Demographic Transition in India
This paper estimates the long-run impact of rural electrification on fertility and maternal health behaviour using NFHS-4 and NFHS-5 microdata. It applies Two-Way Fixed Effects, Instrumental Variables, and Difference-in-Differences approaches based on the rollout of RGGVY. The study finds that electrification reduces fertility and birth incidence through increased media exposure, improved maternal healthcare access, a shift towards institutional deliveries, and lower desired family size.

3. Does Digital Identification Improve Disability Welfare Delivery? Evidence from the Integration of the Unique Disability ID System in India
This paper evaluates whether digital identification improves welfare delivery for persons with disabilities. It exploits the staggered rollout of the Unique Disability ID programme using state-district panel data for 2014–2023 and applies a Difference-in-Differences design. The findings suggest an increase in service intensity and improved targeting in districts where the programme was implemented early.

Academic Projects

4. Lives in Transition: Exploring Livelihoods and Service Access in Aarey Colony
Guide: Prof. Srijit Mishra
This project examined the relationship between infrastructure access and capability deprivation in Aarey Colony, Mumbai. Drawing on the capability approach of Amartya Sen and Martha Nussbaum, it studied access to water, electricity, and basic services among tribal and migrant communities. The project involved field research through in-depth interviews and provided evidence on urban inequality, health vulnerability, and service-delivery challenges.
